# Privacy Notice

_Last updated: April 23, 2026_

## Overview

OpenAgent is an open-source, local-first workflow for using Codex with Obsidian Canvas.

This page covers the website and general product posture at a high level. OpenAgent is designed so the repo, local daemon, and runtime stay on your own machine unless you intentionally connect them to other services.

## Website

This website may be served by standard web hosting infrastructure.

Depending on the hosting provider, normal server logs or basic operational telemetry may exist for reliability, abuse prevention, or debugging.

If you deploy or host OpenAgent yourself, your own hosting setup determines what logs or analytics exist.

## Product posture

OpenAgent is built around a local-first architecture:

- your repo stays on your machine
- the local daemon runs on your machine
- Codex works against your local files
- Obsidian Canvas remains your working surface

If you use the mobile flow, Convos acts as the remote chat interface, but the coding work still runs locally on your computer.

## Third-party services

Your use of Codex, Obsidian, GitHub, Convos, or any hosting provider is also subject to those services' own terms and privacy policies.

OpenAgent itself does not change the privacy behavior of those third-party tools.

## Self-hosting and responsibility

Because OpenAgent is open source and can be run in different environments, the exact privacy and logging behavior depends on how you install, host, and configure it.

You are responsible for reviewing your own runtime, hosting, and integration choices before using OpenAgent with sensitive material.
